FLO

Dashboard glow. Highway hum.

The phone screen pulses white against the windshield — once, twice, three times.

He doesn’t look. Then he looks.

Her — Where are you.

No question mark.

Free — Close.

Her — You said that twenty minutes ago.

He sets the phone face-down. Picks it up again. The city is thinning out. Fewer lights. Longer shadows.

Her — I’m not waiting past midnight.

Free — Then don’t.

Thirty seconds. A minute. The dots appear. Disappear. Appear again.

Her — You don’t mean that.

He doesn’t answer. Foot presses harder.

Her — I left the door open.

He reads it twice. Not because he didn’t understand.

Free — I’m not coming to talk.

Her — I didn’t open the door for conversation.

The phone goes dark. The rearview holds nothing.

Somewhere ahead, a light left on.

He takes the exit without signaling.
