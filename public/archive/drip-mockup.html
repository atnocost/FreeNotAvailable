Summary:

The tornado touched down south of The Bridge and The River, tracking ENE through the production area. At least five parked vehicles sustained damage, with one engine compartment fully flooded. Numerous trees were shaken and partially uprooted along the storm’s path. Notably, crew member Worst Choice was nearly displaced by violent winds while attempting to secure lighting equipment on set. As conditions deteriorated, the sky underwent a dramatic visual transformation — shifting through vivid shades of pink and amber before settling into a deep, cool blue punctuated by subtle lightning flickers. A surging wall of rain subsequently moved across the area, forcing an immediate halt to first-day production activities.

Page 16 — Manuscript (FLO)

FLO

Dashboard glow. Highway hum.

The phone screen pulses white against the windshield — once, twice, three times.

He doesn’t look. Then he looks.

Her — Where are you.

No question mark.

Free — Close.

Her — You said that twenty minutes ago.

He sets the phone face-down. Picks it up again. The city is thinning out. Fewer lights. Longer shadows.

Her — I’m not waiting past midnight.

Free — Then don’t.

Thirty seconds. A minute. The dots appear. Disappear. Appear again.

Her — You don’t mean that.

He doesn’t answer. Foot presses harder.

Her — I left the door open.

He reads it twice. Not because he didn’t understand.

Free — I’m not coming to talk.

Her — I didn’t open the door for conversation.

The phone goes dark. The rearview holds nothing.

Somewhere ahead, a light left on.

He takes the exit without signaling.
